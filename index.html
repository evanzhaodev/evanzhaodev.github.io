About

Dr. Jane Smith

janesmith@university.edu

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at University Name. My research interests include artificial intelligence, machine learning, and human-computer interaction.

I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Massachusetts Institute of Technology in 2020, where I worked on developing new algorithms for natural language processing. Before that, I obtained my M.S. from Stanford University and B.S. from University of California, Berkeley.

Currently, I lead the Intelligent Systems Lab, where we explore the applications of machine learning in healthcare, education, and sustainable development. I teach undergraduate and graduate courses in AI and Data Science.

Projects

Machine Learning for Climate Prediction

This project focuses on developing machine learning models to improve climate prediction accuracy. By combining satellite imagery with ground-based sensor data, we've created a system that can forecast regional climate patterns with up to 30% greater accuracy than traditional models.

Accessible Human-Computer Interfaces

We are designing and implementing new human-computer interfaces that are accessible to people with disabilities. Our current focus is on gaze-tracking technologies combined with predictive text systems to enable efficient communication for people with motor impairments.

View Project Details

Educational AI Assistant

The Educational AI Assistant project aims to develop AI tools that can personalize learning experiences based on student performance and learning styles. Our system analyzes pattern recognition in problem-solving attempts and provides customized guidance to help students overcome specific challenges.

Research

Deep Learning Approaches for Medical Imaging Analysis

This research explores novel deep learning architectures for medical image analysis, with a particular focus on early detection of abnormalities in radiological scans. Our approach has demonstrated a 15% improvement in detection accuracy compared to state-of-the-art methods, particularly for subtle features that are often missed in conventional analysis.

Ethical Considerations in Algorithmic Decision Making

This interdisciplinary research examines the ethical implications of using algorithms for decision-making in high-stakes contexts such as healthcare, criminal justice, and financial services. We propose a framework for evaluating algorithmic fairness and transparency, and recommend policy guidelines for responsible implementation.

Reinforcement Learning for Robotic Control

This research investigates advanced reinforcement learning techniques for robotic control in unstructured environments. We've developed a novel approach that enables robots to learn complex manipulation tasks with minimal human supervision, significantly reducing the training time while improving task performance.
