About

Evan Zhao

hzhao5@uw.edu

Hi! My name is Evan and I am currently a Master's student in Computer Science at the University of Washington, conducting research under the guidance of John Akers and Professor Ira Kemelmacher-Shlizerman. Prior to my Master's, I earned my Bachelor's degree in Computer Science also in University of Washington. My research lies at the intersection of Extended Reality (XR) technologies and Computer Vision, with a particular emphasis on developing novel computational frameworks that enhance user interaction within immersive environments.

Drawing from foundations in machine learning and human-computer interaction, I'm passionate about tackling the fundamental challenges in spatial computing and real-time visual processing for AR/VR applications. I'm hoping to make a contribution in advancing perceptual models that bridge the gap between virtual experiences and real-world environments.

Feel free to reach out to me through email if you want to connect!

Projects

NASA-SUITS-Challenge 2023

For this project, team Astrohuskies designed an AR application for the Microsoft HoloLens 2 to be overlaid within a spacesuit visor to assist astronauts in Extravehicular Activities (EVAs) conducted in outer space environments such as the Moon and Mars. This was done as a part of the 2023 NASA SUITS challenge.
